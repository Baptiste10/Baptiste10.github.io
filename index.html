About

You have just been a victim of Phishing!

You arrived on this page by following the link contained in an email simulating a phishing attack.

This is the first step in an awareness campaign against attacks known as 'Phishing' carried out by NKT. We first want to measure the reaction of our employees to this type of threat in order to establish a basis for comparison with future tests and the results of phishing campaigns.

You may have been surprised by the secrecy of this simulation, which has not been announced. We wanted to prevent our employees from feeling that they were immune to any attack.

Can you keep a secret?

Secret

Do not tell your colleagues yet!

This simulation has not been announced. Why ? Simply because cyber criminals don't warn and announce their attacks. Running this simulation without warning you is the best way to make this exercise as realistic as possible.

So please do not divulge anything to your colleagues at this time: if your colleagues are informed that a simulation is in progress, you risk influencing their behavior and consequently the results of the exercise. We will inform all employees once the simulation is finished and at the latest at the end of September.

We thank you for your cooperation

By making this simulation as realistic as possible, you are helping to set up our Anti-Phishing campaign, which will be supplemented by various communications on the risks and means of protecting yourself.

Is my security compromised?

No. Unlike an actual attack, neither email nor this site will harm your security. In addition, no information about you was collected during this simulation. The exercise was conducted by an external partner who will only share aggregate data.

It is important to note that the ultimate goal of such a simulation is absolutely not to blame but on the contrary to help all employees to adopt appropriate behavior contributing to improving their security and that of NKT.

How can I recognize a phishing email?

Five things should be checked to detect such an attack:

  • The sender's address
  • The content of the email
  • The grammar and arrangement of elements in the email
  • The links contained in the email
  • If you expect this kind of email
